The normal line is the line perpendicular to the tangent line at the point of tangency.


<div className="grid grid-flow-row md:grid-rows-2 md:grid-flow-col gap-y-3 md:gap-0 text-center">
  <div>
    Slope of tangent line
    <BlockMath math="m_{\text{TL}}=f'" />
  </div>
  <div>
    Slope of normal line
    <BlockMath math="m_{\text{NL}}=\frac{-1}{m_{\text{TL}}}=\frac{-1}{f'}" />
  </div>
  <div>
    Equation of tangent line
    <BlockMath math="y-y_1=m_{\text{TL}}(x-x_1)" />
  </div>
  <div>
    Equation of normal line
    <BlockMath math="y-y_1=\frac{-1}{m_{\text{TL}}}(x-x_1)"/>
  </div>

## Examples

Find the equation of the line perpendicular to the function $y=x^3+7x^2+5x-2$ at $\color{fuchsia}{x=0}$.

| Step | | |
| --- | --- | ---
| 1 | Get $y'$. | $y'=3x^2+14x+5$
| 2 | Get $y'$ at $\color{fuchsia}{x=0}$. This is the $\color{lime}{m_\text{TL}}$. | $\color{lime}{m_\text{TL}}=3(\color{fuchsia}0)^2+14(\color{fuchsia}0)+5$<br/>$=\color{lime}5$
| 3 | Get $\color{orange}y$ at $\color{fuchsia}{x=0}$ | $\color{orange}y=(\color{fuchsia}0)^3+7(\color{fuchsia}0)^2+5(\color{fuchsia}0)-2$<br/>$=\color{orange}{-2}$
| 4 | Get the equation of the perpendicular line at $(\color{fuchsia}0,\color{orange}{-2})$. Use the formula. | $y-\color{orange}{y_1}=\frac{-1}{\color{lime}{m_\text{TL}}}(x-\color{fuchsia}{x_1})$<br/>$y-\color{orange}{(-2)}=\frac{-1}{\color{lime}5}(x-\color{fuchsia}0)$<br/>$5y+10=-x$
| 5 | (Optional) Transform to standard form. | $x+5y+10=0$

Find the tangent to $\color{orange}{x^2+y^2=5}$ and parallel to $\color{fuchsia}{2x-y-4=0}$.
| Step | | |
| --- | --- | ---
| 1 | Derive $\color{orange}{x^2+y^2=5}$ with respect to $x$, and get $\color{lime}{y'}$. | $2x+2yy'=0$<br/>$2yy'=-2x$<br/>$y'=\frac{-2x}{2y}$<br/>$\color{lime}{y'=\frac{-x}{y}}$
| 2 | Recall in FNDMATH that two equations of a line are parellel if their constant part is the only thing different. <br/>So, we make a new equation but with an unknown constant. | $\color{fuchsia}{2x-y-4=0}$<br/>$\color{aqua}{2x-y-c=0}$
| 3 | Convert $\color{fuchsia}{2x-y-4=0}$ to the slope-intercept form ($y=mx+b$) to get its slope. | $y = 2x-4$ <br/>The slope is $2$.
| 4 | The slope of the tangent line (a.k.a. $\color{lime}{y'}$) and the slope of its parallel line must be the same. | $\color{lime}{\frac{-x}{y}}=2$
| 5 | We have now 3 equations and 3 unknowns. | $\begin{cases}\color{orange}{x^2+y^2=5}\\\color{aqua}{2x-y-c=0}\\\frac{-x}{y}=2\end{cases}$
| 6 | Solve for $\color{tomato}c$ however you like, then substitute it back to the $\color{tomato}c$ equation. | $\color{tomato}{c=\pm 5}$<br/>$\color{aqua}{2x-y-\color{tomato}{(5)}=0}$<br/>$\color{aqua}{2x-y-\color{tomato}{(-5)}=0}$
| 7 | Final answer: we have 2 equations. | $2x-y-5=0$<br/>$2x-y+5=0$
