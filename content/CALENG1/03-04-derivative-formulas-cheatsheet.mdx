## Definition

$$
f'(x)=\frac{dy}{dx}=y’=\lim_{h\to 0}\frac{f(x+h)-f(x)}{h}
$$

## Rules

$$
\begin{align*}
\text{Constant rule} && \text{Sum \& difference rule} \\
\frac{d}{dx}(n)=0 && \frac{d}{dx}(f \pm g)=f' \pm g'\\
\text{Power rule} && \text{Product rule}\\
\frac{d}{dx}(x^n)=nx^{n-1} && \frac{d}{dx}(fg)=f'g+fg' \\
\text{Constant multiple rule} && \text{Quotient rule} \\
\frac{d}{dx}(nf)=nf' && \frac{d}{dx}\left( \frac{f}{g} \right)=\frac{f'g-fg'}{g^2} \\
\text{Chain rule} && \text{General power rule} \\
\frac{d}{dx}(f(g))=f'(g)g' && \frac{d}{dx}(f^n)=nf^{n-1}f'
\end{align*}
$$

## Trigonometry

$$
\begin{align*}
\frac{d}{dx} (\sin f)&=\cos(f)f' && \frac{d}{dx}(\csc f)=-\csc(f)\cot(f)f' \\
\frac{d}{dx}(\cos f)&=-\sin(f)f' && \frac{d}{dx}(\sec f)=\sec(f)\tan(f)f' \\
\frac{d}{dx}(\tan f)&=\sec^2(f)f' && \frac{d}{dx}(\cot f)=-\csc^2(f)f'
\end{align*}
$$

## Inverse trigonometry

$$
\begin{align*}
\frac{d}{dx}(\sin^{-1} f)&=\frac{f'}{\sqrt{1-f^2}} && \frac{d}{dx}(\csc^{-1} f)=\frac{-f'}{|f|\sqrt{f^2-1}} \\
\frac{d}{dx}(\cos^{-1} f)&=\frac{-f'}{\sqrt{1-f^2}} && \frac{d}{dx}(\sec^{-1} f)=\frac{f'}{|f|\sqrt{f^2-1}} \\
\frac{d}{dx}(\tan^{-1} f)&=\frac{f'}{1+f^2} && \frac{d}{dx}(\cot^{-1} f)=\frac{-f'}{1+f^2}
\end{align*}
$$


## Logarithmic
$$
\begin{align*}
\frac{d}{dx}(\log_nf)&=\frac{\log_n(e)f'}{f}=\frac{f'}{\ln(n)f} \\
\frac{d}{dx}(\ln f)&=\frac{f'}{f}
\end{align*}
$$

## Inverse function *(for this, $g$ is inverse of $f$)*
$$
g'=\frac{1}{f'(g)}
$$

## Exponential function
$$
\begin{align*}
\frac{d}{dx}(n^f)&=n^f \ln(n)f' \\
\frac{d}{dx}(e^f)&=e^ff' \\
\frac{d}{dx}(f^g)&=f^g \left( \frac{gf'}{f}+g' \ln(f) \right)
\end{align*}
$$

## Hyperbolic function
$$
\begin{align*}
\frac{d}{dx}(\sinh f)&=\cosh(f)f' \\
\frac{d}{dx}(\cosh f)&=\sinh(f)f'
\end{align*}
$$
