## General Rectilinear Motion
The velocity of a particle which moves along the s-axis is given by v=2+5t3/2, where t is in seconds and v is in meters per second. Evaluate the displacement s, velocity v, and acceleration a when t = 4 s. The particle is at the origin s = 0 when t = 0. (General Rectilinear Motion)
#### Required: displacement (s)
#### Strategy:
1. Integrate the given velocity formula
2. Use s = 0 and t = 0 to get the constant of integration
3. Input t = 4s to the final displacement formula
#### Solution:
*1. Integrate velocity formula:*

$$ v = 2 + 5t^\frac{3}{2} $$

$$ \int v\space dt=\int(2+5t^\frac{3}{2})\space dt $$

$$ s=\int(2+5t^\frac{3}{2})\space dt=\int2\space dt+\int5t^\frac{3}{2}\space dt=2t+2t^\frac{5}{2}+C $$

$$ s=2(t+t^\frac{5}{2})+C $$

*2. Insert s = 0 when t = 0:*

$$ 0=2(0+0^\frac{5}{2})+C $$

$$ 0=C $$

$$ s=2(t+t^\frac{5}{2}) $$

*3. Input t = 4 s:*

$$ s=2(4+4^\frac{5}{2}) $$

$$ s=72\space m $$

#### Required: velocity (v)
#### Strategy:
1. Input t = 4s to the velocity formula
#### Solution:
*1. Input t = 4 s:*

$$ v = 2 + 5t^\frac{3}{2} $$

$$ v = 2 + 5(4)^\frac{3}{2} $$

$$ v=42\space m/s $$

#### Required: acceleration (a)
#### Strategy:
1. Get the first derivative of the velocity formula
2. Input t = 4s to the acceleration formula
#### Solution:
*1. Get the first derivative:*

$$ v = 2 + 5t^\frac{3}{2} $$

$$ v\frac{d}{dt}=(2+5t^\frac{3}{2})\frac{d}{dt}=2\frac{d}{dt}+(5t^\frac{3}{2})\frac{d}{dt}=0+5(\frac{3}{2})t^\frac{1}{2} $$

$$ a=\frac{15}{2}t^\frac{1}{2} $$

*2. Input t = 4 s:*

$$ a=\frac{15}{2}(4)^\frac{1}{2} $$

$$ a=15\space m/s^2 $$
